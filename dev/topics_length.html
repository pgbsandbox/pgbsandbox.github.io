How long is a topic?

A topic is just long enough to serve its purpose.

Rule of thumb: half a page

A topic, if you were to print it out, would be about a half page long. Sometimes, a topic is slightly shorter or longer but half a page is an ideal length.

Each topic has a very specific purpose: to inform on a very specific subject. If your topic is longer, then it likely isn't serving its purpose and your reader won't find it helpful.

Always err on the side of too short rather than too long.

How do you know a topic is too long?

Generally speaking, you can use these tips to gauge whether you have put too much information in one topic and need to split it into multiple topics, each with a slightly different focus. Any one of these rules means that your topic needs to be chunked a little bit more.
  • If you printed it out, the topic would be 3 or more pages.
  • Your topic has more than any of the following:
    • 3 tables
    • 3 graphics
    • 12 steps
    • 7 paragraphs
    • 5 sections or subheadings
