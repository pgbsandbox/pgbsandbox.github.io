About notes

Notes are parts of your topic that call out information that needs to be noticed.

When to use

Notes can be useful when used only occasionally to call out specific information that you want the reader to notice.

Overuse of notes, such as more than one in a row or more than one in a topic, can have the opposite effect, so be careful to use them only when called for.

Large notes

Notes can contain more than one paragraph, list, or combination of those items.

Note types

A variety of notes types are available to indicate why you are calling attention to a particular piece of information. This helps categorize the information for the reader. Your enterprise may not use all the types.
Attention
Pay extra attention to this note.
Caution
Care is required when proceeding.
Danger
Be aware of this before doing anything else. Could result in injury or death.
Fastpath
A faster way to do the same thing.
Important
Important information that should not be missed. Often, writers decide to either use attention or important, but not both.
Note
Information that should be noted but does not fall into any of the other categories.
Notice
A potential situation which, if not avoided, may result in an undesirable result or state.
Restriction
Something you cannot do.
Remember
Do not forget to do this thing.
Tip
A helpful hint.
Warning
A potentially hazardous situation.
