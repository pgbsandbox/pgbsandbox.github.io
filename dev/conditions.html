Conditions

Conditions may be applied to text to enable filtering of the published content for particular target audiences.

Conditions

Conditions come in logical groups. For example, text may be made conditional for a particular version of a product. We may have conditional content that just applies to version 2 of the product. Or, certain text may only apply to the premium packaging of the product. There may be several packages: standard, premium, ultimate. We see that conditions do come in groups, such as version and package, and that each condition group has its own allowable values.

Currently, AuthorBridge supports viewing conditions applied to text, but it does not support applying conditions to text. Use the Conditions menu panel to access information about the currently applied conditions. You may choose whether or not to have conditions displayed in color.

You may also turn conditions on or off. If a condition is off, it will be filtered from the view. For example, if you turn off the condition for version 2 of a product, then text that applies to version 2 will no longer be visible.

Sometimes conditions are used in combinations. AuthorBridge will choose a unique color for each such combination that exists in a topic. When this happens, the various condition combinations will be shown below a line that separates them from the base conditions. You can only turn base conditions on and off. The combinations will react accordingly.

If the color schem doesn't work well for you, let AuthorBridge compute a new set of colors for you. All you have to do is provide a new name for the color scheme. The same color scheme name will always produce the same colors for a particular set of conditions.
