Apply minimalism to your tasks

Minimalism, when applied to documentation, creates clean, usable procedures.

The idea behind minimalism is that you provide the right information in the right place and with the right format. Everything else is removed (sometimes to another topic), leaving a clean, distilled topic that includes only useful but essential information. Minimalist documentation is easy to follow, quick to read, and amazingly helpful.

You can apply minimalism to all your topic types; however, when you're beginning, it's easiest to apply minimalism to tasks.

  1. Make sure your task is oriented on a user goal.
    If you have content that is important but not directly related to the goal, put it in another topic.
  2. Edit each step so that it contains only the command for the reader to perform.
    Additional information that is pertinent to a particular step can be placed in the instruction section.
  3. Put information that applies to the entire task in the appropriate section.
    Before you begin
    Things the reader must do before they can even start the first step.
    Context
    Why the reader would perform the task. Usually, this is a description of the business goal you are writing for.
    Result
    The result of the entire task.
    Next steps
    What the reader should do after they have finished the task.
  4. Remove all extraneous information.
    1. Edit out words that do not add to the meaning of your content.
    2. Put supporting content in concept or reference topics.
    3. Remove any repetition; you only need to say something once.
  5. Add recovery information.
    When there is a step where readers are likely to run into a problem, add information on how they can recover in the instruction section.
  6. Review the topic heading and introduction to ensure they are still accurate.
