Write a great introduction

Your heading and introduction are a dynamic duo: they perform different functions but complement each other to create an powerful combination.

The introduction section is optional but recommended for all topics, no matter the type. An introduction section is the one section that might not make much sense to you initially but can be very valuable for readers.

A good introduction summarizes the topic and enhances the heading without ever directly repeating anything else in the topic. It is also a great place to add alternate terms or ideas that do not fit in the heading.

In HTML and related published outputs, the introduction is not only searchable (like SEO terms) but it is also part of any links to that topic from other topics and from search results. The reader uses the introduction, combined with the heading, to decide whether to navigate to a particular topic or not.

The better and more accurate your introduction, the more findable your content becomes.

  1. Write your topic.
    The introduction is much easier to write when the topic is complete.
  2. Review your topic heading.
    Does it accurately reflect the scope of the topic? What keywords are missing from the heading that would help readers identify the subject matter of the topic?
  3. Write a one or two sentence introduction that sums up the topic.
    An introduction section summarizes a topic's subject matter concisely and effectively.
