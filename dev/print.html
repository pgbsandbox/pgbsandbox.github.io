Create a PDF

Printing is very basic. One of your print options creates PDFs for you.

You must have Adobe® Reader® installed to create PDFs.

Ordinarily, printing is done downstream by someone who has access to all the topics and has implemented corporate-wide branding on all publications. You would only print a single topic out if you were not sure about length or alignment or to send it for an informal review.

Printing a topic from AuthorBridge approximates how your company might publish. Items that might not be the same include font types, font sizes, margins, pagination, logo, and headers and footers.

You might, for example, print a topic to PDF to send it for review.

  1. Once your content or draft is complete, from Local Files, select Print.
  2. Select your print destination as PDF.
    Your print options depend on what you have set up locally on the machine you are using.
  3. Specify another other print options you want.
    We recommend enabling Headers and footers so that your printout includes a date stamp and pagination.
  4. Select Save.
  5. Save your PDF to a location on your computer.
