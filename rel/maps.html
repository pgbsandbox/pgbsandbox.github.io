How it all comes together

Maps assemble topics for publishing.

Topics as Lego pieces

Your topics are very much like Lego pieces that can be assembled in whatever combination required to create the final, published output needed.

The topics that you and others write are assembled using a map. An advanced writer uses a map to pull together topics in a specific order and hierarchy and then publish to HTML, PDF, and any other required output.

Publishing process

The publishing process uses the content with your markup (the sections you put your content into) plus the underlying architecture to generate smart output, where formatting is applied automatically and systematically.

Similarly, the writer never has to set the font type, size, or any other formatting details of the content. The automated publishing reads what kind of section was used and formats all sections of the same kind (all Notes, for example) the same way.

The automated publishing also automatically generates items that waste a lot of time in traditional publishing, including
  • table of contents
  • volume, section, chapter, and page numbering
  • page breaks and margins
  • headers and footers
  • index
  • list of figures
  • list of tables

The people in charge of publishing can turn on and off the objects they want generated. They can also format them differently for different published files; for example, they can generate a Table of Contents for PDFs but make it collapsible navigation tabs in HTML.

Benefits of automated formatting

The key benefits of automated formatting using semantic markup include
  • creating a consistent look and feel across all content and all writers
  • saving time and effort usually spent formatting, and
  • focusing on the content and the markup.

This separation between content and formatting also means that the source content can be used to generate many formats at once. The content you write in AuthorBridge could end up in PDF, HTML5, ePub, iOS apps, Online Help, or any other formats your readers may need. The formatting will look correct in all those outputs without you ever having to re-work the source content.
