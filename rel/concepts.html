Concepts

Concepts explain and show. Novice readers rely on concepts to gain a fundamental understanding of your product.

Who and why

People who are new to your product will be the most likely consumers of concepts. These novices need a foundational understanding of the subject matter and the underlying abstract ideas.

Readers read a concept to try to understand
  • what things are and what they do, and
  • how things work together.

Examples

Some examples of concepts include:
  • A lengthy definition with an example
  • A diagram that illustrates infrastructure or architecture
  • A description of components of a product
  • An explanation of how components work together

Keeping it conceptual

It is easy to put more into a concept than you should. As soon as you start adding how to information into a concept, such as tips or what buttons to select, you know you've strayed from the purpose of a concept. Anything that tells the reader how to do something belongs in a task instead.
