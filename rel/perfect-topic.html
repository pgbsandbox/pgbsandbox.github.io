Write the perfect topic

The perfect topic is short, concise, and takes twice as long to write as a traditional piece of content.

A topic, even though it's short, takes more effort to write than traditional content. You are doing extra activities, such as focusing on your reader's goal and adhering to the topic types; those activities enhance the topic but take longer to complete. A shorter topic is a better topic but it's not a faster topic.

  1. Identify the goal of the topic.
  2. Create the topic type that matches the purpose of the content.
    Use concepts for explanations; use tasks for procedures and process; use reference for facts and technical details.
  3. Write the content, letting the sections guide you.
  4. Ensure that the topic is not too long and does not stray away from your initial goals.
  5. Write a clear heading that accurately identifies the subject matter for this topic.
  6. Write an introduction that sums up the topic's focus.
    It should enhance but not duplicate the heading.
  7. Edit the content, removing all the extra content that is not absolutely required in this topic.
    If it is still useful, save it for another topic.
  8. Check to make sure you have used the right presentation objects for your content.
    • Tables are easily scanned.
    • Lists can group and clarify.
    • Steps are easy to follow.
    • Paragraphs and graphics can be used to explain complex subjects.
  9. Edit one more time.
    1. Check to make sure you only have one type of information in your topic.
    2. Make sure the content is organized and clear.
    3. Make sure you have used the sections appropriately.
      When something occurs as a result of the step, use the step result.
