Topic types

There are three main topic types: reference, concept, and task.

Why readers access documentation

Readers access the documentation for very specific purposes.
  • To understand what something is, what it contains, or how it works.
  • To learn how to do something.
  • To look up detailed information quickly, such as measurements, requirements, or commands.

Topic types

To align with these purposes, there are three information types that guide you to provide the right kind of information for the reader.

  • Concept: Paragraphs, lists, graphics, and subheadings that let you explain and show.
  • Task: Steps that let you explain how to do something or explain how a process occurs.
  • Reference: Tables, lists, and graphics that help you display detailed information in a quick look-up format.

How to pick the right topic type

When you are writing topics, consider your subject matter and then consider why your readers would be accessing that particular information.
The goal of your topic Topic type
To explain what something is or what something contains. Concept
To tell the reader how to do something or explain how a process occurs. Task
To provide detailed, quick look-up information. Reference

Generic topics

There is a generic topic type called topic that you can use if your content does not easily fit the model for any of the other three topic types. A generic topic should still adhere to the guideline for topic length.
