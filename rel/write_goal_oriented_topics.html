Write goal-oriented content

Content focused on the readers' goals is useful and usable.

Write each topic to anticipate and fulfill a specific reader goal.
  1. Review any user analysis you might have access to.
    Support, Sales, and other customer-facing departments often have information about the users that can be very helpful. Look for information that answers the questions:
    • What kind of users buy your product?
    • What devices and operating systems are they using?
    • What background knowledge are they likely to have?
    • How do they use the product and the features?
    • What confuses them?
    • Where do they tend to run into problems?
  2. Create user scenarios.
    A user scenario is a step-by-step explanation of how specific types of users would actually use product functionality. In the software industry, these are often added to Specifications documents.
  3. Using your user scenarios, create a list of likely topics.
  4. For each topic, decide on the purpose of the content.
    Is it meant to inform? Does it instruct? Is it quick look-up information?
    The answers give you your topic types for each topic.
  5. Write your topics.
  6. Review each topic for an appropriate focus.
    Are you writing for how the reader will use the product, not what the product features are? What does the reader actually need to do?
