Tag words and phrases

It is a great idea to tag words and short phrases to classify them if they have special meaning.

Formatting with superscript, subscript, bold, italic, or underline is not the same as applying a tag. Applying a tag is almost always a better idea.

All other tags indicate the kind of content you are tagging. Choose the tag that describes the type of content.

  1. Highlight the content you want to tag.
  2. From the toolbar, select Add Description.
  3. Select a tag that describes the content or formats it the way you need.
    Tip: More than one tag can be applied at a time. If you do apply multiple tags, use the keyboard LEFT/RIGHT arrows to put your cursor in the place you need.
  4. To modify or remove the classification, click the tagged content.
    • To modify, select the content you want to modify then select the tag button.
    • To remove, select the X.
Tip: If you are tagging words or phrases as you type, use the right arrow on your keyboard to close the tag and continue authoring.
